The CLIMB way

What both studios share

Whichever studio you're in, the method is the same. It's why the two field guides feel like siblings — and why work moves between them.

◆

Real problems, real users

No hypotheticals. You work with actual people who have the problem, and you don't get to skip them.

▲

Learn by doing

Short, just-in-time teaching, then studio time. You build the thing while you learn the tool.

⚑

Gates cleared with evidence

You move forward by defending what's real to your instructor or mentor — not by ticking your own boxes.

📘

A field guide, not a lecture

Each studio has its own Field Guide — the tools, the gates, and a worked example you can hold up as the standard.

↻

Test before you build

Ideas are cheap; tested ideas are valuable. The cheapest test in front of a real person beats months of guessing.

◷

The studio model

You work alongside other teams at different stages, learning from those just ahead and teaching those just behind.
