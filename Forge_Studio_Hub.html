The guide

Learn the tools, then go build

Each tool is a living document, not a form you fill once. Tap a tool to see what it does and how one founder — Pocket Lunch's Maya — actually used it. The fillable worksheets live in the Word field guide (see Resources).

The path warms as you go: cool steel at Discover, through gold and rust, to a green finish at Launch. Move forward only when the gate is cleared — track that under My Progress.

You pass a gate with evidence — not optimism

Gates aren't graded by an instructor. They're checkpoints you walk through with your lead mentor, who pressure-tests your evidence rather than rubber-stamping it. A gate you pass on optimism is a gate that fails you later.

01 · Show

Bring the evidence — conversations, a test result, a number — not a feeling that you're ready.

02 · Defend

Walk your lead mentor through it out loud. If you can't, the gate isn't clear.

03 · Decide

Pass and proceed, or revise and return — with what must be true to pass next time.

Talk to customers first. Test before you build. Show what's real, say it out loud, and clear the gate.

Your lead mentor — your first call

Every founder gets one: a student a few steps ahead who knows these tools. They're your first call when you're stuck, your sounding board before a gate, and your shortcut into the rest of the community.

Show up for these

The community meets all year — none of it required. Founder workshops (one skill at a time), guest entrepreneurs (what actually happened), watch-and-build sessions, and founder check-ins. Drop into whatever serves your venture.

Who it's for — MGNT 250 or not

Forge is the natural next step after MGNT 250, but you don't need it to join. If you're starting cold, Tool 1 gives you everything you need to begin.
